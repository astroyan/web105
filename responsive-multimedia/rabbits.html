Rabbits

There are several species of cottontail rabbits, but the Eastern Cottontail is the most common throughout North America. They are given their name for their stubbed, white tail that resembles cotton. They are primarily crepuscular, or active at twilight due to the low visibility.

Common Name: Eastern Cottontail

Binomial Name: Sylvilagus floridanus

Primary Species Range: North & Central America

Rather than making burrows, Eastern Cottontails make what is called a form, which is more of a slight depression in the ground surrounded with twigs and other bits for camouflage. They primarily eat vegetation but will eat bugs if not much else is available. They prefer to live in clearings that offer places to hide on the side.

Eastern Cottontails live up to a little over a year in the wild if lucky, but there have been rabbits recorded to be five years old. In captivity they generally only live to be nine years old.
