I am originally from the Pocono Mountain region of the Appalachian Mountains. The picture to the left provides a nice illustration of the size of the mountains where I was born.

As far as states go, I have been to, or have driven through, the following: Pennsylvania, New York, Massachussetts, Connecticut, New Jersey, Virginia, West Virginia, South Carolina, North Carolina, Indiana, Ohio, Illinois, Iowa, California, Arizona, and Nevada.

Of all the states and places I've been to in the U.S., I was the most taken with West Virginia. The way that they laid the roads over the mountains astounds me - it almost feels like a roller coaster driving through parts of it.

I moved out to Illinois in October of 2018 to be with my then-boyfriend, now-fiance. The picture to the right is one that I took on one of my many trips out here before I finally moved.

Being from the mountains, one thing that really shocked me out here was the immensity of the sky. I remember the first time I witnessed a thunderstorm in the Midwest; I was so terrified because I could see everything that was going on in the sky! I guess that's a good thing - early warning system.

So far, I have been to the Northeast, Midwest, and Southwest, and in the future I would really like to travel to all parts of the U.S., particularly less populated areas like Montana and the Pacific Northwest.
